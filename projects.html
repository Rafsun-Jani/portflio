Hospital-Based Survey on Occurrence and Management of Diseases of Dogs and Cats at the Veterinary Teaching Hospital, Bangladesh Agricultural University

Research Assistant | Supervised by: Prof. Dr. Md. Taohidul Islam
Funding: Ministry of Science and Technology (MoST), Bangladesh (Project Code: 2023/021)
Duration: Jul 2023 â€“ Jun 2024

Project Overview:
This research aimed to investigate the clinical patterns, seasonal variations, and treatment protocols for commonly encountered diseases in pet animals (dogs and cats) presented at the Veterinary Teaching Hospital (VTH), BAU.

Key Contributions:

  • Participated in the design and execution of a hospital-based epidemiological study covering over 1,000 pet patients.
  • Collected, organized, and analyzed data on animal demographics, clinical signs, diagnostic findings, and treatment outcomes.
  • Conducted clinical examinations and supported the use of diagnostic tools including blood tests, X-ray, and ultrasound.
  • Applied statistical software (IBM SPSS) for chi-square testing and descriptive analysis; used QGIS for visualizing disease distribution across regions.
  • Identified seasonal trends in ectoparasitic infections, prevalence of major pet diseases, and evaluated cure rates of selected treatments.
  • Co-authored and presented a scientific poster at a national technical session to communicate the findings and promote improved veterinary care practices.
