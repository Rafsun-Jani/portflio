Research Experience

Hospital-based Survey on Occurrence and Management of Diseases of Dogs and Cats at the Veterinary Teaching Hospital, Bangladesh Agricultural University

Undergraduate Research Project | Supervised by: Prof. Dr. Md. Taohidul Islam

Funding: Ministry of Science and Technology (MoST), Bangladesh | Project Code: 2023/021

Duration: Jul 2023 – Jun 2024

  • Conducted a comprehensive epidemiological study to assess the clinical frequency, seasonal trends, and treatment outcomes of pet animal diseases (cats and dogs) at the Veterinary Teaching Hospital (VTH), BAU.
  • Collected and analyzed clinical data from over 1,000 pet cases, including demographic information, clinical signs, diagnostic outcomes, and treatment responses.
  • Performed clinical examinations involving vital sign assessment (temperature, pulse, respiration), and supported diagnostic procedures including X-ray, ultrasound, and hematological tests.
  • Utilized statistical software (IBM SPSS) and spatial analysis tools (QGIS) for descriptive statistics, chi-square tests, and disease mapping to detect patterns and associations.
  • Identified seasonal variations in ectoparasitic infections and assessed cure rates for various common diseases using hospital records and follow-up data.
  • Co-authored a scientific poster presented at a national technical session, showcasing impactful findings relevant to pet disease management and veterinary public health.
